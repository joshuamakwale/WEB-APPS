Why Tandiza exists

Zambia's streets hold children who deserve better

Over 75,000 children live on the streets across Zambia's urban centers — in Lusaka, Kitwe, Ndola, Livingstone, and beyond. Many go entire days without a proper meal, without shelter, without hope.

Tandiza steps in with immediate relief — nutritious meals, clean water, and basic care — while working toward long-term solutions through education support and community rebuilding.

Every Kwacha donated goes directly to a child in need. No layers of bureaucracy, no hidden costs. Just impact.

"I had not eaten for two days when Tandiza found me. They gave me food, they gave me dignity. Now I go to school."
— Chisomo, age 11 · Lusaka

What we do

Our Programs

🍲
Daily Feeding

Hot, nutritious meals served every morning and evening at our feeding points across Lusaka and surrounding areas. No child turned away.

🏫
Education Support

School uniforms, stationery, and fees for children who wish to return to formal education. Learning opens every door.

🏥
Health & Care

Basic medical check-ups, vitamins, and hygiene kits. A healthy child is a child who can dream and grow.

🏠
Safe Shelter

Temporary safe spaces for children, especially during rainy season and cold nights, with blankets and warm clothing provided.

👪
Family Reunification

Working with social services to safely reunite children with family members when possible, rebuilding ties that matter.

💧
Clean Water

Access to clean drinking water and sanitation at all our feeding centers, because water is life — amanzi ngumbilo.

Make a difference today

Give the gift of a full stomach

ZMW 3 feeds one child for a day. ZMW 90 feeds a child for an entire month. ZMW 1,000 sponsors a child's full year of meals and education.

Every donation — no matter the size — lands directly in the hands of a child who needs it. We publish transparent monthly reports so you can see exactly where your money goes.

You can donate via mobile money (Airtel Money, MTN MoMo), bank transfer, or international card. Tandiza is a registered Zambian charitable trust.

Bwalya found a reason to smile again

After losing both parents, Bwalya spent months on the streets near Soweto Market. Tandiza volunteers found him malnourished and frightened. Today he attends Grade 3 at a local school, receives daily meals, and dreams of becoming a doctor.
